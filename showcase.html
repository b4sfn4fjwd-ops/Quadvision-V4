The problem

“Complete the square” is where students give up.

It’s taught as a sequence of symbol-pushing rules — halve b, square it, add to both sides, factor — with no picture of why. Students memorise the steps, forget them under exam pressure, and never see that the same idea is the birthplace of the quadratic formula itself.

Our novelty

The square is real — and the student builds it.

Most apps animate the steps. QuadVision makes the geometry literal: the x² term is a real square, the bx term is a strip that splits in half and wraps two sides, and the missing corner is a tile you physically drag into place. The phrase “completing the square” finally means what it says.

  • Watch the square form, tile by tile, beside its live parabola.
  • Build it yourself by dragging the strips and corner into position.
  • Diagnose classic mistakes in worked solutions — learning from error, not just avoiding it.
  • Play timed sprints and boss battles that turn repetition into motivation.
  • Track growth on a live dashboard of mastery, accuracy and badges.
The learning science

Every feature is grounded in how people actually learn.

🧱

Concrete → abstract

CRA sequence

The Concrete–Representational–Abstract approach moves learners from physical tiles, to a drawn model, to symbols. Our build-and-watch tools are the concrete and representational stages the textbook skips.

👁️

See it and read it

Dual coding

Pairing a visual (the square, the parabola) with words and symbols builds two memory traces instead of one — making recall stronger and more durable.

🔍

Learn from error

Productive failure

Spotting and explaining a wrong line trains the exact self-checking judgement exams demand. Misconceptions are confronted directly, not hidden.

🎮

Practice that sticks

Gamified mastery

XP, levels, combos and badges sustain the repeated retrieval that builds fluency. Mastery is tracked per skill, so practice targets real gaps.

🌐

Learn in your language

Bilingual access

Full English ⇄ Bahasa Melayu switching lowers the language barrier so cognitive effort goes to the maths, not the translation — vital in Malaysian classrooms.

♿

Built for everyone

Universal design

Colourblind-safe palettes, adjustable text, high-contrast and dyslexia-friendly modes, reduced-motion and keyboard support mean no learner is designed out.
